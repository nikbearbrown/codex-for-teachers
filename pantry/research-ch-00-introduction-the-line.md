## 2. The Core Concept — State of the Field

### What is settled

- **K-12 CS teachers are time-pressured and under-prepared for AI-assisted build work.** CSTA 2025.
- **The teacher-as-builder model is pedagogically sound.** Education research (Resnick, Papert, Kafai).
- **Reflective practice deepens both teaching and craft.** Dewey, Schön.

### What is disputed

- **Whether teachers can become builders without significant developer skill.** Book takes the position: yes, with conducting discipline (specification + verification, not deep coding).
- **Whether Codex is the right tool for K-12 teachers vs. simpler interfaces.** Framework applies to any agentic tool; Codex is current implementation.

### What has changed recently (last 5 years)

- The Codex app (macOS Aug 2025; Windows March 2026) is the current 2026 surface. Multi-agent parallel execution, scheduled Automations, Skills, Codex Security.
- Anthropic Education Report (2024–2025) and OpenAI engineer-voice posts (Dec 2025) are the new empirical/practitioner anchors.

## 6. Pedagogical Delivery Research

### Prior knowledge required
ChatGPT for lesson plans (Claude.ai or ChatGPT familiarity, not Codex).

### Common misconceptions to disarm
- **"AI for teachers means lesson plans."** No. Builds, not content.
- **"Codex is better ChatGPT."** Agentic system. Different stakes.
- **"I need to be a developer."** Conducting requires specification + verification.
- **"My students know more than me."** Partially true; not relevant. Teacher-who-has-built knows what student doesn't: cost of skipping discipline.

### Effective instructional sequences
- **Reflective-practice framing from page one.**
- **Concrete builds, not abstractions.** Name three Acts upfront.
- **CSTA 2025 as validation.** Don't moralize.
- **Dictation shortcut sidebar.** Win+H needs verification (TIKTOC OQ-7).

### Known failure modes
- **"Extra work" frame.** Frame as time-saver.
- **Over-claiming Seth's authority.** Co-practitioner.
- **Terminal intimidation.** CSTA 2025 is reassuring.

### What separates understanding from memorization
A reader who *understands* Ch 0 can articulate why the book has both halves per chapter. A reader who memorized Ch 0 lists the three Acts without grasping the rationale.
