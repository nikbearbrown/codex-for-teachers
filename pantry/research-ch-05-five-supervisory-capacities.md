## 2. The Core Concept — State of the Field

### What is settled

- **Human-machine task division has 60+ years of literature.**
- **Pattern recognition and judgment are different operations.**
- **Naming improves deployment.**

### What is disputed

- **Five as right number.** Operational choice.
- **Stability under tool improvement.** TIKTOC: "currently requires human judgment."
- **Distinguishability.** PA/IJ commonly conflated.

### What has changed recently (last 5 years)

- Anthropic 2026 RCT validates engagement-pattern framework.
- Codex's autonomous execution makes capacity exercise MORE important.

## 6. Pedagogical Delivery Research

### Prior knowledge required
Act One framework. Teacher starting grading-tool build.

### Common misconceptions
- **"Five things on every prompt."** Each fires at different moment.
- **"Conscious performance every time."** Fuses with practice.
- **"PA = paranoia."** Vigilance.

### Effective sequences
- **Define in grading-specific language.**
- **Trace through a build.**
- **Diagnostic exercise.**

### Known failure modes
- **Glossary chapter.** Lead with action.
- **Over-specification.** Usefully fuzzy.

### What separates understanding from memorization
A reader who *understands* Ch 5 reads unfamiliar Codex transcript and labels capacities. A reader who memorized Ch 5 recites five names.
