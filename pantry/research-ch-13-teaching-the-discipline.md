## 2. The Core Concept — State of the Field

### What is settled

- **PCK is real and identifiable.** Education research since Shulman 1986.
- **Teacher-built artifacts have pedagogical value beyond use.**
- **Process assessment ≠ product assessment.**
- **Specification-writing as core CS literacy** (emerging 2025–2026 consensus).

### What is disputed

- **Build logs at K-12 scale.** Heavier than product; pedagogically richer.
- **Whether to grade build logs.** Open.
- **Sharing teacher artifacts verbatim.** Guidance on redaction.

### What has changed recently (last 5 years)

- 2024–2026 AI-assisted student work forces new assessment practices.
- Specification-writing as CS literacy is a 2025–2026 conversation; the book is in it.

## 6. Pedagogical Delivery Research

### Prior knowledge required
Teacher finished three builds.

### Common misconceptions
- **"Teaching is just assigning the students book."** No. Teacher's PCK matters.
- **"My artifacts are too personal."** Redaction.
- **"Build logs are too much."** Peer review reduces teacher load.
- **"Students won't see my framework as authority."** They will if you've lived it.

### Effective sequences
- **Chapter map table.** 14 rows; ongoing reference.
- **Teacher's AGENTS.md as classroom walkthrough.**
- **Build-log assessment design.**
- **Teacher's-role concrete questions.**

### Known failure modes
- **Students-book-summary.** It's a map.
- **Over-claiming teacher's AGENTS.md authority.** One teacher's lessons.
- **Build-log fetishism.** One tool among options.

### What separates understanding from memorization
A reader who *understands* Ch 13 has prepared redacted AGENTS.md for sharing and designed a build-log assessment. A reader who memorized Ch 13 recites table without using.
